About Budapest

Budapest, the capital of Hungary, is a stunning city bisected by the River Danube. Known for its rich history, grand architecture, and vibrant nightlife, Budapest offers a unique blend of old-world charm and modern energy. From the historic Castle Hill to the lively ruin bars of the Jewish Quarter, there’s something for every traveler.

Top Attractions

  • Buda Castle: A UNESCO World Heritage Site with stunning views of the city.
  • Fisherman’s Bastion: Fairytale-like lookout towers overlooking the Danube.
  • Széchenyi Thermal Bath: One of Europe’s largest and most famous thermal spas.
  • Parliament Building: A neo-Gothic masterpiece on the banks of the Danube.
  • Heroes’ Square: A grand plaza celebrating Hungary’s national history.

Food & Drinks

Hungarian cuisine is hearty and flavorful. Don’t miss local favorites like gulyás (goulash), lángos (fried bread with toppings), and sweet treats like dobos torte. For drinks, try a glass of Tokaji wine or enjoy Budapest’s famous ruin bars in the Jewish District.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: Spring and Autumn.
  • Currency: Hungarian Forint.
  • Language: Hungarian, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas.
  • Transport: Excellent public transit—use the metro, trams, and buses.
  • Safety: Generally safe, but watch out for pickpockets in crowded spots.
